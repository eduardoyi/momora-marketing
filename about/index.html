We built this because our baby book is blank.

Before we had kids, we gave baby books as gifts.

We thought they were adorable. A thoughtful way to say “you’ll want to remember every little thing.”

Fast forward two years and two babies later.

Our own baby book? Completely empty. Not a single “first giggle” or “favorite food” filled in. Just pristine pages silently judging us from a shelf somewhere under a pile of laundry.

Turns out, when you have a toddler who thinks sleep is optional and a baby who believes socks are a snack, documenting anything feels impossible. We were lucky if we remembered what day it was, let alone the first time someone said “mama.”

So we stopped pretending we’d ever have time for glue sticks and scrapbook paper, and we built something we could actually use — in real life, with real chaos.

Meet Momora: made by two tired parents for every other tired parent.

It’s the app we wish existed when our first was born: a quick, guilt-free way to jot down a tiny moment before it disappears — and have it turned into something beautiful automatically.

No pressure. No “perfect parent” energy. Just your real, messy, wonderful life, remembered.

Because the truth is: you’ll forget more than you think you will.

And that’s okay — it means you were busy living it.

We just wanted to make something that helps you hold onto a few of those small, extraordinary seconds.

The giggles, the “pasketti” mispronunciations, the way they said “bye” to the moon.

Those are the memories worth keeping.
